Burt Munro "The World's Fastest Indian"

Herbert James "Burt" Munro

Burt Munro was the subject of a 2005 film, The World's Fastest Indian, based on a composite of his Bonneville speed runs. The film shows an old man who continues to believe in his dream despite the difficulties of life. His dream is to beat the speed record with his motorcycle, an Indian Scout. To get there, he will live his passion day and night.

This film shows us that a man's passion can move mountains. No matter what others think about our dream, the important thing is to believe in ourselves. I have a dream and you?

Some dates

1899
Born in Invercargill, New Zealand.
1915
Burt bought his first motorcycle, a Douglas.
1919
He bought a Clyno motorcycle and went to the Fortose racing circuit, where he set some speed records with it.
1920
He sold his Clyno and bought his famous Indian Scout. The motorcycle was in stock condition, with a 600 cc engine and 3 speed transmission.
1936
The Velocette come to complete his garage.
1938
His first speed record in New Zealand on his Indian motorcycle.
1962
World land speed record at the Bonneville Salt Flats by getting his Indian motorcycle up to 178.97 miles per hour.
1967
New speed record for the sub 1000 cc motorcycle class at the Bonneville Salt Flats.
1978
Burt died of natural causes
